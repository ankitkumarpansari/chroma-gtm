## ğŸŸ¡ Cohort 3: Competitor Customers

### Definition

Companies **currently using a competitor's vector database**. They've already solved the "do I need a vector DB?" questionâ€”now it's about switching to Chroma.

### Who's in This Cohort?

| Competitor | Company Profile | Pain Points to Target |
|------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Pinecone** | Well-funded startups, enterprises | Cost at scale, vendor lock-in, closed-source |
| **Weaviate** | OSS-first companies, mid-market | Operational complexity, scaling challenges |
| **Qdrant** | Developer-focused startups | Limited enterprise features, support |
| **Elasticsearch/OpenSearch** | Enterprises with existing Elastic | Performance, cost, complexity for vectors |
| **PGVector** | Companies using Postgres | Performance at scale, limited features |
| **MongoDB Atlas Vector** | MongoDB shops | Not purpose-built, performance |
| **Milvus/Zilliz** | Enterprises, especially APAC | Complexity, support quality |

### Company Profiles by Competitor

#### Pinecone Customers
> **Profile**: Well-funded startups and enterprises who chose the "safe" option
- **Why They Chose Pinecone**: Market leader, enterprise sales, perceived safety
- **Why They Might Switch**:
  - Cost surprises at scale
  - Desire for open-source foundation
  - Better accuracy/performance needs
  - Frustration with closed ecosystem
- **Approach**: Focus on cost savings, accuracy benchmarks, open-source benefits

#### Elasticsearch/OpenSearch Customers
> **Profile**: Enterprises with existing Elastic infrastructure adding vector search
- **Why They Chose Elastic**: Already using it, "good enough" for vectors
- **Why They Might Switch**:
  - Vector search is bolted-on, not native
  - Performance issues at scale
  - Operational burden
  - Cost of Elastic Cloud
- **Approach**: "2x faster, 10x cheaper, purpose-built for vectors"

#### Weaviate/Qdrant Customers
> **Profile**: Developer-focused companies who chose open-source
- **Why They Chose Weaviate/Qdrant**: Open-source, developer-friendly
- **Why They Might Switch**:
  - Need managed service with better support
  - Scaling challenges
  - Missing enterprise features
- **Approach**: "Same OSS flexibility + enterprise-grade managed service"

#### PGVector Customers
> **Profile**: Companies trying to avoid adding new infrastructure
- **Why They Chose PGVector**: Already using Postgres, simplicity
- **Why They Might Switch**:
  - Performance hits wall at scale
  - Limited vector-specific features
  - Need hybrid search, filtering
- **Approach**: "Purpose-built performance, easy migration from Postgres"

### How We Find Competitor Customers

| Source | Signal | Quality |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **YouTube Videos** | Company mentioned in competitor tutorials | High |
| **Case Studies** | Featured on competitor website | Very High |
| **Job Postings** | "Experience with Pinecone/Weaviate" required | High |
| **GitHub** | Using competitor SDK in public repos | Medium |
| **Conference Talks** | Presenting about their competitor stack | High |
| **Blog Posts** | Writing about their vector DB choice | High |

### Competitor Relationship Status

| Status | Description | Approach |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Evaluating** | Actively looking at alternatives | High priority, fast follow-up |
| **Open** | Not actively looking but open to conversation | Regular nurture, share content |
| **Satisfied** | Happy with current solution | Long-term nurture, wait for trigger |
| **Locked-In** | Multi-year contract, deep integration | Very long-term, track contract renewal |

### Why They Matter

- **Validated need**: Already using a vector DB, understand the category
- **Easier conversation**: No need to explain "why vector DB"
- **Competitive intelligence**: Learn what competitors do well/poorly
- **Higher ACV potential**: Often larger, more mature companies

  - 5-20 projects per year
- **Value to Chroma**:
  - Volume of implementations
  - Technical expertise (can self-serve)
  - Startup/mid-market reach
- **What They Need**:

- **Value to Chroma**:
  - Recurring revenue per customer
  - Product integration (sticky)
  - Co-marketing opportunities
- **What They Need**:
  - Embedded/OEM pricing
  - White-label options
